What is an ISA( Income Share Agreement) ?

Posted by admin / Leave a comment

If you are someone who has been surfing the internet a lot and have been looking for some online courses you most probably might have come across the term ISA or Income Share Agreement. You must be thinking what does it even mean? Well to be honest it is a fairly new concept in the online learning sector and the name itself gives away a general idea of what it means.
An ISA is a financial structure where two parties mutually agree one of which provides something of value and the other agrees to share a small percentage of their income for a specific period of time. This in simple terms means that you don’t necessarily need to pay the fees upfront to the institution or organization and can easily pay them in small part payments later from your monthly salary.
This model has been trending lately because it minimizes the risk involved and avoids the students from drowning in debt to pursue education.

How is it better than a loan?

  • A loan requires you to pay the principal amount back which you will be taking for college fees along with the added interest which takes a big chunk out of your pocket and students generally drown in it and are not able to pay it back even after a long time. Meanwhile ISA only requires the students to pay the fees in installments through their salary and is only activated when the candidate has a job and there are no hidden charges. On the other hand the loan along with its interest is required to pay back no matter what the circumstances are.

  • The bank doesn’t care whether or not you get a job and only cares about the repayment of their loan.

  • On the other hand the ISA is drafted by the company providing you the educational course and the ISA is their potential investment in you so it becomes their priority to get you placed at a great company.

Is ISA risky?

ISA is a form of payment of fees just like any other mode of payment. In most of the scenarios it is optional and you can pay your fees upfront if you can afford to. It is just a provision for the students so that they don’t go through the stress of paying the fees.So with that said, ISA IS OPTIONAL and you can choose whether or not to avail its benefits.
